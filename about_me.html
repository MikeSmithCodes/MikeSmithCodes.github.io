In the Classroom

My undergraduate degree was in Aerospace Engineering. Iowa State University was a great school and offered me a lot of opportunities to tailor my education. I was quickly drawn to aircraft competitions where colleges around the world compete to design, build, and fly model aircraft. Scoring using centered around maximizing the weight of the payload while minimizing the empty weight of the airplane. Two lessons stuck with me. First, as you as you start committing to a design, you start to feel the pain from any compromises you needed to make. That said, if you try to optimize every detail, you won't have a plane to fly on the day of competition. Second, what works on paper or in simulations needs real world validation. I learned a lot every year and felt these extracurricular projects complemented my coursework well.

My graduate degree was in Computer Science. Georgia Tech was a great school for very different reasons. At Georgia Tech the courses were largely predetermined and coursework itself was less collaborative. The coursework was challenging, especially with a full-time job, but the topics were interesting. I have revisited a few of my projects since graduating to make updates.

Spare Time

My happy place is in front of a mitre saw with Eric Church on the speakers and a cold drink not too far from the tape measure. I spend a lot of time in front of computer screens so it is nice throw some saw dust around the garage and build an appreciation for physical problems like how the grain direction or humidity might impact a table top's surface over time.

As a girl dad, my tools have spent more time idle while I play "school" and "family." This isn't how I thought I would spend my years but it really is the best. Sweeping the floor 100 times a day while stepping on toys I just picked up is a small cost to pay for the joys of fatherhood.

Finally, getting outside is great and I kick myself for not doing more of it. I enjoy anything on or near the water, biking, and playing sports.
