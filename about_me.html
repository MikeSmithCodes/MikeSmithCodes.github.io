About Me

In the Office

I am drawn to hard problems and get bored when the work is too repetitive. For that reason, I've become a bit of a jack of all trades but they say that is better than a master of one. I prefer to build skills that are widely applicable and relate to first principles. I am also a people person. I like building relationships with the people I interact with and find the work more enjoyable when you can chat about families or trips in the hallway between meetings. I've worked in the aviation, space, automotive, and healthcare industries. The diversity of those experiences has helped me a lot in terms of quickly spotting trends that are productive versus problematic and it has helped me communicate with technical and non-technical groups no matter their background.

In the Classroom

My undergraduate degree was in Aerospace Engineering. In addition to the coursework, I led a number of teams representing the school in competitions to design, build, and fly model aircraft. Scoring usually centered around maximizing payload weight while minimizing the empty weight for the airplane. Two lessons stuck with me. First, as you as you start committing to a design, you immediately feel the pain from any compromises you needed to make. That said, if you try to optimize every detail, you won't have a plane to fly on the day of competition. Second, what works on paper, or in simulations, needs real world validation.

My graduate degree was in Computer Science. Most of the coursework touched on various aspects of Artificial Intelligence. I learned a lot and still go back and improve on some of the projects. In particular I enjoyed building models to solve puzzles and games, predicting stock market behavior, and determining the location and optimal paths for autonomous vehicles. I could spend lifetimes on these problems and never be bored. Recent advancements in these areas only spark a greater interest too.

Spare Time

Not much beats making a pile of saw dust on a Fall day. I've built a handful of tables and enjoy every step of the process. It is easy to overlook the complexities of even a simple project as you get into woodworking but you quickly learn there is a lot of consider. What wood and materials should you use? What direction should the grain go? What is the temperature and humidity of the shop? There is a real sense of pride when the project ends too.

I do not get outside enough, but when I do, I enjoy every second. I like anything on or around the water, biking, and playing sports.
